# Article Workshop

Use this skill when the user wants to workshop an article collaboratively,
headline by headline or section by section, especially when the goal is to
make it less generic without adding junk content.

## Workflow

1. Confirm whether the user wants to start from an outline, a draft, or a
   specific section.
2. Work on one headline or section at a time.
3. For each section, identify:
   - its job in the article
   - whether it already says something concrete
   - what feels generic, repetitive, or thin
4. Prefer the smallest useful improvement:
   - keep it as-is
   - tighten it
   - add one concrete example, contrast, or consequence
   - cut it if it does not earn its place
5. Preserve the article's voice and intent unless the user asks to change them.
6. Avoid adding content just to make the article longer.
7. After each section, report the decision and ask whether to continue to the
   next one.

## Editing Rules

- Prefer high-signal specifics over filler.
- Do not introduce new claims, examples, or arguments unless they support the
  section's purpose.
- If a section already works, say so and leave it alone.
- If a section feels generic, look first for a concrete scenario, a precise
  claim, or a clear contrast.
- Keep paragraphs short if possible, but make each one do real work.

## When To Stop And Ask

Pause for user input when:

- the section's purpose is unclear
- adding support would require new facts or sources
- the user wants a stronger rewrite than a targeted edit
- the article needs a structural change rather than a local improvement

## Boundaries

- Do not use this skill for proofreading only.
- Do not use this skill for rewriting an entire article in one pass unless the
  user explicitly wants that.
- Do not optimize for length, SEO, or novelty at the expense of clarity.
- Do not broaden one section into multiple unrelated ideas.
